# AV Distribution / Translation Enablement Team – Summary

## Purpose

The AV Distribution team exists to prepare, package, and support English‑source audiovisual materials so that global translation teams can efficiently and accurately produce vernacular-language videos. These videos are used on JW.org, in Kingdom Hall meetings, at conventions, and for Bible study. The team functions as the bridge between content creation and worldwide translation.

## Core Mission

To make English media **translation‑ready** by providing reliable, timely, and well-structured materials, while supporting translation teams and absorbing complexity so that translators can focus on language and delivery rather than technical obstacles.

## What the Team Actually Does

The team performs five tightly connected functions:

### 1. Pre‑Translation Media Preparation

- Review original sound track (OST) elements for translatability
- Rework and compose OST text using MEPS Composition
- Create structured audio templates (Audacity) for translation recording
- Integrate composed OST elements into video using After Effects
- Perform basic video cleanup and alignment

### 2. Packaging and Distribution

- Prepare English/master video and audio packages
- Upload all master materials into MediaCenter (internal CMS)
- Configure MediaCenter containers so languages can upload vernacular audio and OST
- Ensure MediaCenter outputs transcode correctly to final MP4s

### 3. Translation Reference Materials

- Produce reference scripts (primarily for orientation rather than direct translation)
- Maintain subtitles where required
- Enter and verify metadata used downstream by translation teams

### 4. Quality and Risk Management

- Proofread OST text, scripts, and metadata
- Identify technical and delivery issues before release
- Proof‑watch all videos prior to publication (current practice)
- Catch upstream technical omissions or errors that require fixes before translators begin work

### 5. Scheduling and Support

- Schedule release dates and publication timing
- Manage large spikes (especially conventions)
- Provide technical and workflow support to translation teams worldwide
- Respond to ad hoc requests from Translation Services and other departments

## How Work Flows (Simplified)

1. Audio Video Services (AVS) notifies the team of upcoming projects and scope
2. Materials are delivered after filming/editing (with early OST review where possible)
3. Work is scheduled internally
4. Media preparation and packaging take place
5. Proofing and validation occur across multiple artifacts
6. Materials are uploaded and configured in MediaCenter
7. Final videos are proof‑watched and published or scheduled
8. Ongoing support is provided to translation teams

## Workload Characteristics

- Baseline: ~25 products per month
- Predictable but intense spikes:
  - Conventions: ~20 projects per month (Dec–Apr)
  - Recorded conventions: ~45–50 projects (May–Jun)
- Additional special campaigns and schools throughout the year

Projects fall into:

- ~30% standard (repeatable)
- ~30% variants (minor changes)
- ~40% special or one‑off

Currently, all project types move through largely the same pipeline.

## Key Structural Realities

- Many people touch each project, largely to manage risk late in the process
- Proofing often catches subjective or non‑blocking issues
- Media packagers frequently identify technical problems originating upstream
- The team often absorbs downstream costs from late changes or ad hoc requests
- Demand enters through multiple informal channels with limited visibility

## Team Identity and Challenges

- The team experiences an identity gap between its name (“AV Distribution”) and its real function
- Support work was added later, increasing scope without a clear mandate reset
- Roles evolved over time, some by necessity rather than design
- Longstanding habits exist due to historic pressure to “just get things out”

## Strengths

- High expertise and commitment to quality
- Strong spiritual motivation and sense of responsibility
- Deep understanding of translation needs
- Willingness to absorb complexity for the benefit of others

## Current Improvement Direction

The team is moving toward:

- Clearer definition of purpose and mandate
- Better alignment with end‑user (translator) needs
- Reduced firefighting through clearer structure and expectations
- Safer ways to question legacy steps and remove work that no longer adds value

---

This document reflects the current reality of how the team operates, serving as a baseline for organisational improvement, process redesign, and clearer communication with oversight and partner departments.
